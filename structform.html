Overview

About the company

The Structural Group of Companies encompasses four construction divisions that have been providing a full range of concrete services for over 50 years. Over the decades, the Structural Group has earned a solid reputation within the construction industry and has been involved in some of the most notable building projects in Canada, the United States and the Caribbean.

The Problem

The client was not satisfied with their existing website since it didn’t reflect the quality, look and reputation of their company. Not only was it visually unappealing, it was also missing components, had poor navigation and lacked organization.

Personas

Understanding the user

To gain a better understanding of the target audience, I created a persona that demonstrated the users that would be visiting the Structural Group’s website. The persona helped me keep the target audience in mind as I conceptualized my design solutions. Users need the website to be informative and showcase the company’s accomplishments in order for them to want hire the Structural Group.

Usability testing

Recruiting users to test the original website

To determine the specific issues with the existing website, I conducted moderated usability tests where I recruited 5 users to navigate through the original website. I gave them a series of tasks to accomplish and asked them questions about the sites organization, navigation and visual design. My main goal was to determine the major pain points and issues that users faced so that I could design better solutions for the new website.

Questions:

  1. Do you find it easy to navigate through the site?
  2. What would you rate the websites organization out of 5?
  3. What are 3 words you would use to describe the sites visual design?
  4. Do you think the websites use of space is efficient?
  5. Is anything about the website frustrating?

Test Results

Top insights determined by the usability test

After all 5 users completed the usability test, I determined the following issues with the website:

  1. 80% of users found it frustrating to navigate the website since they had to continually hover over the “Menu” tab to view navbar options, there was also no “Home” option in the menu.
  2. 60% of users rated the sites organization 2/5 and 40% rated it 3/5. They didn't like how each page used only a small portion of the page to display text.
  3. Many described the website as cluttered, inconsistent, tacky and unattractive.
  4. Poor use of background images, typography and designs were inconsistent.
  5. Contact page was very messy and information was displayed as an image rather than HTML.
  6. No back button when viewing a member of the team.
  7. Broken pages like the “Affiliates” page which contained no text.
  8. Missing background images which made text difficult to read.
  9. Projects were displayed in a list which wasn’t engaging or appealing to the user.
  10. Not responsive; the content of the site does not render well on different device screen sizes.
  11. Poor use of screen space, there was a lot of empty unused space and text was limited to a small, fixed-size box.

Overall the major issues of the website were:

1. Poor Organization

An organized website allows users to easily navigate and find the information they need quickly. The existing website had poor navigation and lacked organization making it difficult for users to move through the site.

2. Ineffective Design

Visually appealing and functional websites enhance the user experience and leave the users wanting to return. The design of the current website was inconsistent and jeopardized its trust with users.

Ideation

Solving the existing website's problems

Improving Organization

  1. Implement a fixed navbar so users can easily navigate without further scrolling.
  2. Create a “Projects” section so users can visually understand the company’s services.
  3. Display individual projects as cards instead of a list to make it more engaging and visually appealing.

Improving Design

  1. Follow a design scheme with a strict set of colours, typography, icons and buttons.
  2. Ensure there are no missing components such as images.
  3. Use Bootstrap4 and media queries to make the website responsive.
  4. Ensure that screen space is optimized to prevent visuals from looking cluttered.

Competitive Analysis

Getting inspiration from competitor companies

Once I had an understanding of the new sites organization and structure, I visited the websites of similar companies to gain inspiration and get the feel of these types of businesses. I explored the websites of Ellis Don, Blackwell, Eastern Construction, Minto and PCL. Comparing the Structural Group’s original website to these made it clear that a lot of visual improvements needed to be made. A notable difference was the use of space; the client’s website did not optimize screen space and looked overall tacky and unprofessional compared to its competitors. I also kept in mind the way the competitors displayed their projects; instead of simply listing the project names, they included a photo and caption.

Usability Testing

Testing the final prototype

After prototyping the high-fidelity wireframes in Adobe XD, I conducted another usability test on the improved design. This was done to determine if the pain points of the original website were eliminated. With the same 5 users, I asked them to go through the new website and rate the navigation, organization, responsiveness and visual design.

Development

Developing the website

Once the high-fidelity wireframes were completed and approved by the client, I began developing the website. I used HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, Bootstrap4 and Subversion to develop the site and ensure it was responsive across all device screens. I implemented animations, micro-interactions and added scroll behaviour to the website to make it more interactive. I'm currently developing more pages and communicating with the client to gather further details and ensure the website is meeting all requirements.
